Study Debrief

This study investigates whether process transparency in a neural audio codec affects how much users trust the system. Each session asked you to rate the system in isolation, based only on what you heard and saw in that session.

  • System Alpha — showed you process information about how it compressed your audio (waveforms, spectrograms, quality metrics, and a plain-language summary).
  • System Beta — returned the processed audio without showing those process details.

Both systems used the same underlying neural compression technology (Meta's EnCodec). Your trust ratings can be interpreted in relation to process transparency, while your audio quality ratings help check whether perceived fidelity stayed comparable.
