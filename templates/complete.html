Study Debrief

This study investigates whether making the inner workings of a neural audio codec visible and explainable affects how much users trust the system. You experienced two types of systems:

  • System Alpha — showed you detailed information about how it compressed your audio (waveforms, spectrograms, quality metrics, and a plain-language summary).
  • System Beta — processed the audio silently, without providing any additional information.

Both systems used the same underlying neural compression technology (Meta's EnCodec). Any differences in your trust ratings reflect the effect of explainability, not differences in audio quality.
